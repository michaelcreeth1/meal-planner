# 00 — Product Brief

## Working title

**Family Meal Planner**

Alternate internal name: **Family Meal Translator**

## Problem statement

Parents of picky eaters often cook under a difficult set of constraints:

- Children accept only a limited core set of foods.
- Accepted foods are presentation-specific: plain chicken may work, saucy chicken may fail.
- Children may reject foods that are mixed together even if they accept the ingredients separately.
- Parents want adults and children to share main ingredients rather than cooking separate dinners.
- Parents want to gently expand their children's accepted foods without making dinner a nightly battle.
- Meal planning becomes cognitively expensive because every meal requires thinking through adult version, kid version, prep timing, fallback foods, and exposure opportunities.

The app reduces that cognitive load.

## Product thesis

The app should help parents plan meals by translating adult meals into kid-specific, deconstructed plates using shared ingredients and gradual exposure strategies.

It should answer questions like:

- What meals can I make this week that my kids might actually eat?
- How can I make a grown-up meal while keeping kid plates simple?
- What should I pull out before saucing/spicing/mixing?
- What is a reasonable tiny exposure for each kid tonight?
- Which foods are gaining traction over time?
- Which foods should I pause because they are causing conflict or burnout?

## Target users

Primary user:

- Parent or caregiver cooking dinner for a family.
- Has one or more picky or selective children.
- Wants adults to eat interesting food.
- Wants kids to share ingredients with adults when possible.
- Often plates deconstructed versions for kids.
- Has limited time and mental energy at dinner.

Secondary users:

- Partner/spouse who needs to know what worked before.
- Caregivers who occasionally cook for the children.
- Parents of children with strong texture or sauce/mixing preferences.

## Core principles

### 1. Adult meal first, kid translation second

The app should avoid defaulting to bland kid meals. It should help adults make real meals, then derive kid-compatible plates.

### 2. Shared ingredients over separate dinners

Suggestions should maximize overlap between adult meal components and kid plate components.

### 3. Presentation matters

The app must distinguish food from format. A child may accept chicken strips but reject shredded chicken in sauce.

### 4. Deconstruction is a first-class feature

The app should treat separate components, sauces on the side, raw vs cooked vegetables, and toppings as core planning primitives.

### 5. Exposure should be gentle and optional

The app should recommend tiny, low-pressure exposures: smell, touch, lick, dip, one small piece, or simply seeing the food on the table.

### 6. Parent speed matters

Logging should take seconds. The app should not require detailed journaling after dinner.

### 7. AI should be structured, not chatty

AI outputs must be valid structured data that the app can render, save, and reuse.

## Success criteria

The product is successful if it helps parents:

- Choose dinners faster.
- Cook fewer separate meals.
- Serve kid plates with less friction.
- Reuse family ingredients more effectively.
- Identify bridge foods and meal patterns that work.
- Slowly expand the children's food comfort zones.
- Remember what worked without relying on memory.

## Key differentiator

Most meal apps ask: "What recipe do you want?"

This app asks:

> Given my actual kids, what adult meal can I make, how do I plate it for each child, and what should I try next?

## Example product output

Adult meal:

> Chicken shawarma rice bowls with cucumber, yogurt sauce, pita, herbs, and pickled onions.

Kid 1 plate:

> Plain chicken pieces, rice, cucumber sticks, pita, yogurt sauce on side.

Kid 2 plate:

> Rice, pita, cucumber, one tiny try of chicken.

Prep strategy:

> Pull kid chicken before adding extra spice. Keep toppings separate. Put sauce in a dip cup.

Why this works:

> Shares chicken, rice, cucumber, and pita. Adult flavor comes from toppings added after plating.

Risk level:

> Bridge.
